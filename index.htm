Profile

I am a researcher at the Department of Applied Linguistics, Translation and Interpreting at Saarland University where I am affiliated with Project B1 (Information Density and Scientific Literacy in English – Synchronic and Diachronic Perspectives) in the DFG-funded SFB 1102. For this project, I am comparing scientific writing to other discourse types from the 17th century to the present through a corpus analysis of linguistic densification as it manifests itself in the noun phrase across different historical stages.


I obtained an interuniversity MPhil in cognitive and functional linguistics in 2014 from KU Leuven, Ghent University, Vrije Universiteit Brussel and University of Antwerp and an MA in English linguistics and literature in 2013 from KU Leuven where I was advised by Hendrik De Smet and Liesbet Heyvaert respectively. Between February and June 2014, I interned at the Quantitative Lexicology and Variational Linguistics (QLVL) research unit at KU Leuven where I collected and annotated corpus data for the FWO-funded project Exploring probabilistic grammar(s) in varieties of English around the world with Benedikt Szmrecsanyi. I completed a BA in applied English linguistics at Alexandria University back in 2006.
