Profile

I am a PhD student and research assistant at the Department of Applied Linguistics, Translation and Interpreting at Saarland University where I am affiliated with Project B1 (Information Density and Scientific Literacy in English – Synchronic and Diachronic Perspectives) of the DFG-funded SFB 1102 under the supervision of Elke Teich. For this project, I am using an information-theoretic approach to compare scientific and general English writing from the 17th century to the present through a synchronic and diachronic corpus analysis of linguistic densification as it manifests itself in the noun phrase across different historical stages.


I obtained an interuniversity research MA in cognitive and functional linguistics in 2014 and an MA in English linguistics and literature in 2013 from KU Leuven under the direction of Hendrik De Smet and Liesbet Heyvaert respectively. Between February and June 2014, I interned at the Quantitative Lexicology and Variational Linguistics (QLVL) research unit at KU Leuven where I collected and annotated corpus data for the project Exploring probabilistic grammar(s) in varieties of English around the world under the guidance of Benedikt Szmrecsanyi. I completed my BA in applied linguistics at Alexandria University back in 2006.
