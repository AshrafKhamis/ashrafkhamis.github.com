Profile

I am a research assistant at the Department of Applied Linguistics, Translation and Interpreting at Saarland University where I am affiliated with Project B1 (Information Density and Scientific Literacy in English – Synchronic and Diachronic Perspectives) in the DFG-funded SFB 1102. For this project, I am using an information-theoretic approach to compare scientific and general English writing from the 17th century to the present through a synchronic and diachronic corpus analysis of linguistic densification as it manifests itself in the noun phrase across different historical stages.


I obtained an MPhil in cognitive and functional linguistics in 2014 and an MA in English linguistics and literature in 2013 from KU Leuven where I was advised by Hendrik De Smet and Liesbet Heyvaert respectively. Between February and June 2014, I interned at the Quantitative Lexicology and Variational Linguistics (QLVL) research unit at KU Leuven where I collected and annotated corpus data for the FWO-funded project Exploring probabilistic grammar(s) in varieties of English around the world with Benedikt Szmrecsanyi. I completed my BA in applied linguistics at Alexandria University back in 2006.
