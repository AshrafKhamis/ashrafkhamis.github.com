Profile

I am a seasoned technical writer specializing in user manuals and context-sensitive help systems for software and hardware products. I am backed by over eight years of professional experience extending far beyond the scope of technical writing to include localization management, linguistic research, web content development, marketing communications, and customer service.


In my daily work, I adopt a topic-based authoring approach with the goal of creating both structured and unstructured documents that are concise, consistent, and accessible to the end user. I thrive in an agile development environment where writing is iterative and incremental, feedback cycles are short, and cross-functional collaboration is paramount. I enjoy working independently as much as I do in a team, having developed and maintained a diverse client base for over four years as a freelance writer and editor of commercial web content, B2B case studies, white papers, technical HR reports, software manuals, and legal and business books.


As a linguist, I worked on research projects funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG) and Research Foundation Flanders (FWO) and published in the areas of corpus linguistics, sociolinguistics, language variation and change, and syntax. I hold an interuniversity MPhil in cognitive and functional linguistics from KU Leuven, Ghent University, Vrije Universiteit Brussel, and the University of Antwerp, an MA in English linguistics and literature from KU Leuven, and a BA in applied English linguistics from Alexandria University.
